## What Types of QR Codes Can You Generate?

**Quick answer:** QR Gamified Studio supports 10 QR code types out of the box — URL, vCard, WiFi, Email, Phone, SMS, Calendar Event, MeCard, Plain Text, and X/Twitter Profile. Each encodes data differently so the scanner's device knows exactly what to do.

Most people think QR codes are just for URLs. They're not. A QR code is a container for structured data, and the format of that data tells the scanning device whether to open a browser, save a contact, join a WiFi network, or compose an email.

Here's the full breakdown.

---

## URL QR Codes

The most common type. Encode any web address and the scanner's browser opens it automatically.

```text
https://qr.gamified.studio
```

Best for: marketing materials, product packaging, business cards, restaurant menus.

One detail people miss: always include `https://` in your URL. QR scanners handle it better than bare domains, and it ensures the link works on every device.

---

## vCard QR Codes

**Quick answer:** vCard QR codes encode a full digital contact card — name, phone, email, company, address, and more. Scanning one adds the contact directly to your phone's address book.

vCards follow the [vCard 3.0 specification](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6350) and can include:

- Full name, first/last name
- Multiple phone numbers (work, mobile, home)
- Multiple email addresses
- Company and job title
- Physical address
- Website URL
- Notes

This is the most data-dense QR type. For bulk generation (conferences, teams), use the [bulk vCard tool](/vcard/bulk) to generate hundreds of QR codes from a CSV file.

---

## WiFi QR Codes

**Quick answer:** WiFi QR codes let anyone join your network by scanning — no typing passwords. They encode the SSID, password, and encryption type.

The encoded format looks like this:

```text
WIFI:T:WPA;S:MyNetwork;P:MyPassword;;
```

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| `T` | Encryption type | `WPA`, `WEP`, `nopass` |
| `S` | Network name (SSID) | `MyNetwork` |
| `P` | Password | `MyPassword` |
| `H` | Hidden network | `true` (optional) |

Perfect for offices, Airbnbs, cafes, and events. Print the QR code once and guests connect instantly without asking for the password.

---

## Email QR Codes

Encode a pre-filled email with recipient, subject line, and body text. When scanned, the device's default email client opens with everything filled in.

```text
mailto:hello@example.com?subject=Hello&body=I%20found%20your%20QR%20code
```

Use cases: feedback collection, support requests, event RSVPs. The subject and body are optional but dramatically increase response rates when pre-filled.

---

## Phone and SMS QR Codes

Phone QR codes trigger a direct call:

```text
tel:+1234567890
```

SMS QR codes open a pre-filled text message:

```text
SMSTO:+1234567890:Hey, I scanned your QR code!
```

Both are useful for customer support lines, emergency contacts, or quick-response marketing campaigns.

---

## Calendar Event QR Codes

**Quick answer:** Event QR codes encode iCalendar data — title, location, description, start/end times. Scanning adds the event directly to the user's calendar app.

The format follows the iCalendar (`.ics`) standard:

```text
BEGIN:VEVENT
SUMMARY:Team Standup
LOCATION:Conference Room B
DTSTART:20260315T090000
DTEND:20260315T091500
END:VEVENT
```

Great for conferences, meetups, classes, and any event where you want attendees to save the date without manual entry.

## MeCard vs vCard

MeCard is a simpler, more compact contact format. It encodes less data than vCard but produces smaller QR codes — which scan faster and work better on printed materials at small sizes.

| Feature | vCard | MeCard |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Standard | RFC 6350 | NTT DoCoMo |
| Data density | High | Medium |
| QR code size | Larger | Smaller |
| Photo support | Yes | No |
| Multiple phones | Yes | No |
| Best for | Full contacts | Quick sharing |

Use vCard when you need a complete contact profile. Use MeCard when size matters — small stickers, badges, or business cards where the QR code needs to be compact.

## Customization Options

Every QR code type supports the same visual customization:

- **Colors**: Foreground, background, and corner colors
- **Patterns**: Dots, rounded, classy, extra-rounded
- **Corner styles**: Square, extra-rounded, dot
- **Logo**: Upload and embed your brand logo
- **Frame rounding**: Adjust border radius
- **Transparent background**: For overlaying on designs

All QR codes can be downloaded as PNG or SVG. SVG is recommended for print — it scales to any size without losing quality.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What QR code type should I use for business cards?

vCard is the best choice for business cards. It encodes your full contact info — name, phone, email, company, title, address — and saves directly to the scanner's address book. If your card is small and space is limited, MeCard produces a more compact QR code with the essential fields.

### Q: Do QR codes expire?

No. The QR codes generated by QR Gamified Studio are static — the data is encoded directly in the image. They never expire, require no account, and work offline. The only way a QR code "breaks" is if the destination URL goes down (for URL-type codes).

### Q: Can I generate QR codes programmatically?

Yes. QR Gamified Studio includes an [MCP server](/docs/mcp) that integrates with AI assistants like Claude, plus a CLI tool for batch generation. Both support all 10 QR code types with full customization options.
